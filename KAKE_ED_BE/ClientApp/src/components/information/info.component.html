Department Information

1. Arrival


On arrival to the Emergency & Trauma Centre you will be seen by the Triage Nurse to assess your condition so we can send you to the right area to be treated in the department. More serious cases will be given priority.

Once seen by the Triage Nurse you will be seen by a member of your Administration team to ensure we have your contact details such as Medicare and private health status, your local doctor and next of kin.

Our Administration Officer can also help with issues relating to charges that will be incurred by non-Medicare card holders.

2. Treatment


Once you have seen the Triage Nurse and Administration Officer you will be sent to your Resuscitation Area, Acute Area or Fast Track Area.
Investigations and treatments will determine if you need to be admitted or can go home.
These can take some time. Please ask if you are unsure what is happening or your condition gets worse whilst waiting.

Please tell the Emergency & Trauma Centre Staff about:

- Any health problems you have
- Medications and treatments you take
- If you are pregnant or breastfeeding
- Allergies
- If you may be infectious to others or have been exposed to someone with an infectious illness
- If you have recently travelled overseas

3. Outcome


Most of our patients will get to go home. If you do have to be admitted it can take some time for a hospital bed to be ready. Until then you will be cared for by Emergency & Trauma Centre staff.

Time Spent in Emergency Departments

This section shows data related to presentations (visits) to this hospital’s emergency department. Each visit is counted separately, so the number of presentations may not be equal to the number of people seen or treated by the emergency department if some people visited more than once. Here, ‘patients’ refers to the number of presentations.

Time spent by all patients in the emergency department In 2017–18, the median length of time for all patients to depart this hospital’s emergency department was 3 hours 3 minutes. The length of time until most patients (90%) had departed was 7 hours 56 minutes, compared to its national peer group performance of 8 hours 14 minutes.
